I · Change · Prototyping

Theme (quick cue):
Exploration & rapid learning

Prototyping lets architects and teams explore high-risk ideas in isolation, building quick throw-away spikes to validate concepts without polluting the main codebase. Early feedback loops reveal technical or business pitfalls, so decisions rest on real data rather than assumption. By separating experimental code into disposable branches or sandbox environments, you preserve production integrity and avoid accruing long-term debt. When a prototype succeeds, its learnings guide a clean, well-structured implementation; when it fails, you discard or pivot without fear. This practice accelerates innovation while safeguarding stability, ensuring that only validated patterns merge into the core architecture.

The Prototype — As a Major Arcana embodiment of experimentation, this card reminds architects that even small experiments shape strategic direction. When drawn alongside I of Change, it urges you to treat prototypes as first-class artifacts: document learnings, measure against objectives, and align quickly before investing deeper.

The Vision — This Major Arcana highlights the importance of connecting every prototype to a larger roadmap. It prompts you to ask: Does this spike advance our north-star goals, or is it a distracting diversion? By referencing The Vision, you ensure rapid experiments remain tied to strategic alignment and shared purpose.
