II · Change · Estimation

Theme (quick cue):
Slicing work, anchoring forecasts

Estimation is about breaking down complex features into bite‐sized increments that can be compared to past work and adjusted as risks surface. You create ranged forecasts—best‐, likely‐, and worst‐case scenarios—to set realistic expectations and accommodate uncertainty. By continuously refining estimates against actuals, you maintain trust with stakeholders while preserving the flexibility to pivot. Visible tracking of delivered scope against original projections sharpens your understanding of hidden assumptions and highlights areas where unknowns must be reduced. Ultimately, good estimation fosters a culture where teams commit confidently and adjust course based on measurable feedback loops.

VI The Roadmap — This card underscores that accurate estimation underpins effective sequencing. As you plot milestones and capacity, forecasting becomes a living artifact; emerging risks force you to revisit estimates and reorder dependencies. Roadmap clarity emerges when estimation drives realistic deliverables rather than aspirational targets.

X The Debt — When hidden technical trade‐offs and shortcuts accumulate, estimates balloon unpredictably. Debt reminds you to factor legacy burdens into sizing exercises. Recognizing accumulated compromises helps you adjust forecasts—allocating extra buffer for pay‐down work—and prevents unforeseen overruns from blindsiding the roadmap.
