III · Change · Planning

Theme (quick cue):
Sequencing and dependency planning

Planning orchestrates sequencing of tasks, aligning capacity, risk, and dependencies to turn vision into reality. The architect decomposes high-level goals into milestones, mapping out critical paths and resource constraints. By keeping plans living documents—regularly updated with feedback and changing assumptions—the team preserves flexibility while maintaining stakeholder trust. Effective planning surfaces hidden dependencies between teams and technologies early, allowing proactive mitigation of bottlenecks. Clear planning reduces optimism bias, setting realistic timelines and measurable checkpoints. Architects facilitate plan reviews, injecting technical context into roadmap discussions to ensure sustainable delivery, balancing near-term wins with long-term architectural health. Ultimately, robust planning guides iterative progress without calcifying assumptions or neglecting emergent risks.

VI The Roadmap — As the architect aligns sequencing, the Roadmap card underscores structuring interdependent tasks into clear phases. It reminds you to revisit assumptions when variables shift, ensuring that milestone planning adapts to technological or stakeholder changes without breaking the overarching timeline.

IX The ADR — This card highlights the importance of recording architectural choices and their rationale. Under Planning, ADR ensures that all decision points—trade-offs, alternatives, and risks—are captured, providing context for future course corrections and enabling transparent reviews as the project unfolds.
