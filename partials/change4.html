IV · Change · Scaling

Theme (quick cue):
Scaling

As demand grows, you architect for horizontal expansion—breaking services into stateless components, sharding data stores, and enabling autoscaling groups. Observability guides each step: metrics reveal bottlenecks so you can incrementally add capacity or refactor hotspots. By planning for elasticity early, you avoid costly rewrites and ensure resilience under load, balancing performance, cost, and complexity as the system evolves.

VI The Roadmap — When planning scaling phases, the Roadmap clarifies dependency order and resource allocation. It ensures you sequence infrastructure upgrades before traffic spikes, aligning teams on timelines and preventing surprises when capacity must grow.

XVIII The Risk — Scaling introduces new failure modes: untested shards, autoscaling misconfigurations, or cascading cache misses. The Risk card reminds you to threat-model these scenarios, set SLOs for new components, and build fallback paths before traffic peaks expose weaknesses.
