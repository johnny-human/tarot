V · Change · Legacy Entanglement

Theme (quick cue):
Inherited complexity, stability vs inertia

Legacy Entanglement highlights the invisible knots formed over years of quick fixes, outdated frameworks, and untracked dependencies. An architect should map these seams, write characterization tests to understand hidden behaviors, and choose a strangler pattern rather than a big-bang rewrite. By isolating legacy components behind clear interfaces, teams can incrementally decouple without disrupting ongoing feature work. This principle encourages thoughtful planning for refactoring—balancing risk, cost, and continuity—so that stability is maintained while technical debt is gradually paid down. The ultimate goal is a clear path forward that prevents brittle entanglement from grinding future innovation to a halt.

The Legacy — This card underscores the weight of inherited systems; its presence forces reflection on why certain complexities persist. It reminds architects to balance respect for stability with courage to challenge inertia, ensuring that valuable history isn’t preserved at the cost of future agility.

The Migration — When migration appears alongside Legacy Entanglement, it signals the necessity of carefully planned movement. It encourages architects to choreograph incremental transformations—moving data, APIs, or entire modules step by step—so that modernisation occurs without catastrophic downtime or loss of institutional knowledge.
