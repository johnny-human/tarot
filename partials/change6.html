VI · Change · Tech Debt

Theme (quick cue):
Accumulated trade-offs, invisible burdens

Tech debt arises when teams trade long-term maintainability for short-term delivery. An architect must catalogue these compromises in a visible backlog, allocate budget for gradual pay-down, and measure “interest” as slowed lead time. Without vigilance, undocumented hacks and design shortcuts compound into cryptic code paths that impede future features, inflate defect rates, and frustrate onboarding. By treating debt like a balance sheet—tracking its principal, estimating payoff effort, and scheduling continuous refactors—the architect ensures debt remains a strategic lever rather than a hidden drag on velocity and quality.

X · The Debt — As the Major Arcana embodiment of cumulative burdens, The Debt card warns when hidden trade-offs have become systemic. When Tech Debt appears below it, architects confront not only code smells but the deeper cultural acceptance of shortcuts—inviting a disciplined payoff plan before collapse.

XVI · The Refactor — In tandem with Tech Debt, The Refactor major urges continuous, incremental improvement. It reminds architects that paying down debt isn’t a one-off crusade but a rhythmic practice: small, safe code surgeries that restore integrity and prevent future accrual of hidden costs.
