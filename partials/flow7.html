VII · Flow · Conflict Resolution

Theme (quick cue):
Harnessing tension for progress

Architects frequently sit between feuding priorities—performance versus scope, time-to-market versus robustness, team A’s roadmap versus team B’s refactor. This card reminds us that healthy conflict, when channelled, exposes hidden assumptions and sharpens design. The practice is deliberate: surface dissent early, frame discussions around shared goals, separate people from problems, and record decisions so debate does not rewind. Genuine resolution produces clearer boundaries, tighter interfaces, and renewed trust; avoidance breeds shadow work and brittle compromises. Treat disagreement as diagnostic data, not a threat, and guided tension becomes a forge where better architecture is hammered out.

VIII The Stakeholder — When this major appears beside Conflict Resolution, it urges the architect to translate technical dispute into shared business language. By naming the real interests at the table, you transform opponents into partners seeking a mutually visible win, not just a louder microphone.

XI The Disruption — Pairing with Conflict Resolution, The Disruption warns that smouldering friction can burst into innovation or chaos. Lean in: facilitate a safe “storming” phase, let bold ideas clash, and harvest the resulting creative sparks before they scorch delivery timelines.
