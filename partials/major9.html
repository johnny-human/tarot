IX · The ADR

Theme (quick cue):
Architectural Decision Records – clarity over chaos

Element / Suit:
Water — Flow

An Architectural Decision Record (ADR) captures a moment of architectural intent, the alternative paths considered, and the consequences anticipated, preserving context long after whiteboards are wiped. It acts as a lightweight contract between past and future engineers, preventing déjà-vu debates and making rationale traceable to code. By writing ADRs at the time of decision, teams expose hidden assumptions, integrate stakeholder voices, and link implementation details to strategic vision. The card reminds architects that clarity is currency: capturing even imperfect reasoning is better than relying on memory and folklore. An ADR is memory, map, and mirror in one concise document.

Structure (Earth) — An ADR anchors design artifacts, giving diagrams and code a naming hinge. When the structure drifts, the corresponding record flags intent, guiding refactorings back toward the agreed domain boundaries without reinventing rationale.

Flow (Water) — Writing ADRs norms conversational flow: propose, debate, decide, document. The ritual turns heated meetings into paced currents, letting knowledge glide downstream to new hires and upstream to sponsors who crave assurance of thoughtful governance.

Risk (Fire) — Each record extinguishes uncertainty by capturing trade-offs and fallout, but also exposes combustible corners. Reading a chain of ADRs reveals patterns of risky bias, prompting proactive security reviews before the spark jumps into production.

Change (Air) — When requirements shift, an ADR becomes a pivot note: supersede, not erase. This living lineage of decisions allows architecture to evolve transparently, acknowledging past wisdom while inviting future adaptability without losing narrative continuity.
