I · Risk · Threat Modeling

Theme (quick cue):
Map dangers, design defenses

Threat modeling brings the unseen dangers of a system into plain sight. By cataloging assets, actors, and attack paths, architects convert vague anxieties into actionable design constraints. The exercise forces clarity: boundaries sharpen, data flows emerge, and hidden assumptions surface for inspection. With prioritized mitigations, teams shift security from after-thought to continuous discipline. Revisiting the model each iteration keeps pace with changing features and dependencies, ensuring defenses evolve alongside functionality. Done well, it produces shared diagrams, ranked risks, and clear owners—turning abstract fear into a living contract between architectural ambition and operational safety.

XVIII The Risk — This card magnifies uncertainty, urging architects to confront exposure head-on. Paired with Threat Modeling, it transforms raw anxiety into structured vigilance: see the flames before they spark, and design escape routes that turn looming catastrophe into controlled challenge.

XV The Outage — Speaking from the 3 a.m. pager wake-up, it shows the cost of blind spots. Together, these cards form a loop: envision the breach, prevent the call, and harvest lessons when prevention fails, hardening the system for the next cycle.
