III · Risk · SLAs and SLOs

Theme (quick cue):
Reliability targets, error budgets

Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Service Level Objectives (SLOs) transform vague reliability expectations into explicit, numeric commitments. By defining error budgets and latency ceilings, architects anchor engineering choices in customer impact: every backlog item, deployment, and rollback must respect the agreed allowance. Healthy SLOs act as guardrails, pausing feature work when burn rates spike and granting creative freedom when budgets are healthy. They foster shared visibility across product, operations, and leadership, replacing gut feel with actionable telemetry. When revisited regularly, these targets evolve with usage patterns, preventing both over-engineering and complacency. Without them, risk conversations drift and accountability blurs. Ultimately, clear SLAs and SLOs transform uptime from guesswork into governance.

XV The Outage — When XV The Outage appears beside this card, it warns that numbers alone cannot prevent failure. Use the error budget as a siren: when it hits zero, declare incident readiness, rehearse runbooks, and treat the deficit as technical debt due now.

XIII The Governance — Drawn with XIII The Governance, SLAs and SLOs become policy levers. Publish targets in executable contracts, tie release gates to budget burn, and let automated dashboards conduct the orchestra of compliance. Good governance turns reliability metrics into a shared, enterprise-wide language of accountability.
