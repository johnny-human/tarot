V · Risk · Zero-Day

Theme (quick cue):
Critical vulnerability, race to patch

A Zero-Day is a flaw no one planned for yet every attacker covets. Architecture that respects this card assumes breach: bill-of-materials scans run nightly, dependencies stay pinned, and rollback scripts wait in the wings. When advisory sirens sound, automated pipelines slice approval queues, canary clusters soak-test patched images, and feature flags darken risky paths without human hesitation. Communication is as vital as code; incident channels light up with clear roles, and a single commander tracks blast radius, customer impact, and legal notice windows. Success is measured by how briefly the window stays open—and how calmly the team closes it.

The Outage — This card reminds us that a Zero-Day unpatched is already an outage in waiting. Reading them together urges architects to rehearse the fire drill today, so tomorrow’s pager buzz feels like choreography rather than chaos.

The Refactor — Pairing with the Refactor reframes the fix as more than a hot patch. It is a chance to excise brittle code, upgrade libraries, and harden boundaries, turning emergency toil into lasting design improvement.
