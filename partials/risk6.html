VI · Risk · Overengineering

Theme (quick cue):
Overly complex solutions

Overengineering happens when elegance gives way to elaboration—layers, frameworks, and abstractions appear “just in case” rather than “just in time.” Each extra knob widens the blast-radius, slows onboarding, and bloats cognitive load. The architect’s discipline is to ask “Which risk am I really buying down?” and to pay only that premium. By favouring clear language, observable constraints, and reversible steps, you create systems that can grow organically instead of fossilising in prematurely sophisticated scaffolding. Simplicity is not laziness; it is strategic reserve for the problems that truly earn complexity.

XI · The Disruption—Every ornate castle invites the storm; disruptive change exposes how excess layers block pivot speed. This card reminds you that innovation favours nimble structures and punishes baroque fortresses.

VII · The Monolith —The monolith’s mass warns that building too much into one shape can freeze evolution. Overengineering often hides inside a single powerful core that later resists the very flexibility it promised to enable.
