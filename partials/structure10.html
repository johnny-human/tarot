X · Structure · Hexagonal Architecture

Theme (quick cue):
Ports & adapters isolate domain logic

Hexagonal Architecture seats pure business rules at the centre and surrounds them with “ports” that declare everything the core needs from the outside world. Concrete technologies—databases, message buses, UIs, vendor APIs—arrive later as “adapters” that plug into those ports without poking holes in the domain. The pattern invites fast, in-memory testing, easy tech swaps, and parallel team work: one squad can craft adapters while another evolves the core without merge-storm collisions. By inverting dependencies, it shrinks blast radius, clarifies ownership, and lets the code express business truth instead of framework trivia—ideal for evolving products and regulated systems alike.

The Container — Like a well-built container image, hexagonal boundaries package the domain, promise reproducibility, and keep noisy neighbours out. They remind us that runtime details may change, but a sealed contract preserves portability and trust across every cluster we deploy.

The Architect — This card asks: have you drawn ports that mirror the business language or the framework manual? True vision balances clarity with flexibility, ensuring adapters serve strategy rather than dictating it. The Architect blesses designs where policy never leaks protocol.
