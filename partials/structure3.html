III · Structure · Coupling

Theme (quick cue):
Dependency gravity & looseness

Coupling measures how tightly one component depends on another, dictating how painlessly the system can evolve. Healthy architecture minimises direct knowledge of concrete implementations, instead trading through stable interfaces, message queues, or events. By mapping dependency graphs, enforcing inversion of control, and avoiding cyclical references, you keep change localized and regression risk low. Loose coupling lets teams deploy independently, test in isolation, and replace modules without ripple effects; tight coupling, by contrast, amplifies every tweak into a coordinated release and slows innovation. Continuously examining integration points and refactoring toward seams keeps the codebase supple under growth and shifting requirements today.

VII The Monolith — Evoke The Monolith when coupling grows unchecked; its massive, unified core warns that convenience often hides future inertia. The card urges you to weigh the security of a single source of truth against the bottleneck of synchronized releases and the fear of widespread failure.

XVI The Refactor — Invoke The Refactor as a remedy: it champions incremental decoupling, interface extraction, and test-guided change. Drawing this card signals momentum for slicing dependencies, embracing adapters, and restoring autonomy so separate modules can evolve, scale, and even be replaced without a high-stakes rewrite.
