IV · Structure · Cohesion

Theme (quick cue):
One purpose per module

Cohesion asks every class, function, or bounded context to tell a single clear story. When related responsibilities sit together, the codebase feels like a well-edited book: each chapter flows logically, intent is obvious, and change ripples stay inside a tight circle. High cohesion reduces cognitive load for newcomers, flattens defect-finding time, and supports parallel work because teams rarely step on each other’s toes. Low cohesion, by contrast, scatters concepts across files and layers, forcing developers to hop back and forth to understand even a minor tweak. Pursuing cohesion therefore multiplies velocity while quietly raising quality and morale.

The Architect — Cohesion embodies the Architect’s quest for structural clarity: every element must sit where its intent is obvious. When you inspect a cohesive module, you glimpse the Architect’s blueprint made real, and design discussions shift from “where does this live?” to “does this belong at all?”.

The Refactor — Cohesion fuels effective refactoring: tighter units mean smaller, safer cuts and quicker feedback. The Refactor card reminds us that revisiting code is painless when concepts are already clustered; splitting tangled duties then feels less like surgery and more like tidying a well-labeled workshop.
