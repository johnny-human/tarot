VII · Structure · Design Patterns

Theme (quick cue):
Reusable solutions  •  shared vocabulary

Design patterns give engineers a shared language to describe proven solutions to recurring design challenges. By naming structures like Strategy, Observer, or Adapter, they compress complex intent into a single, well-understood term, shrinking explanations and review time. Patterns also highlight trade-offs, steering conversations toward behaviour and variability instead of low-level code. Used judiciously, they impose just enough structure to keep code extensible while avoiding over-engineering. The architect’s job is to select patterns that fit the domain, document their rationale, and enforce consistency across modules. When each pattern is clearly recognised, collaboration accelerates, onboarding shortens, and future refactors proceed with confidence.

The Architect — As The Architect oversees the system’s blueprint, Design Patterns become their shorthand wiring diagram. Drawing this card beside Patterns reminds the team to balance elegance and practicality, ensuring every chosen idiom serves the wider vision rather than mere intellectual curiosity.

The Refactor — The Refactor appears when structures drift from intent. Paired with Design Patterns, it urges pruning ceremonial layers, restoring pattern purity, and verifying tests still guard contracts. Together they signal evolution: keep the vocabulary, renew the code, and drop abstractions that no longer earn their keep.
